Conservation

Located in Seattle, Washington, the Seattle Aquarium is a waterfront aquarium sitting across two piers and inspiring conservation efforts for the local coastal community. They take a partnership-focused approach that looks at long-term conservation strategies to work directly in species rehabilitation, scientific research, public policy advocacy, and building community. Their main focus areas are local to the Washington coast in the Salish Sea and to a part of the Indo-Ocean called the Coral Triangle where they have been a part of supporting abalone, leopard sharks, and more.
