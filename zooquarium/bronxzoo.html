Conservation

The Bronx Zoo is a zoo home to more than 11,000 animals in the middle of Bronx, New York. They are responsible for creating the Wildlife Conservation Society (formerly the New York Zoological Society) which is a non-profit organization working on conservation for many different wildlife species. Their focuses include rescue, rehabilitation, and breeding programs for animals like American bison and Kihansi spray toads. They are also home to scientists that are at the forefront of modern medicine for zoonotic diseases. Through these things, the Bronx Zoo operates on a global scale and has one of the most critical impacts on wildlife conservation in the world.
