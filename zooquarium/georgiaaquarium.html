Conservation

Located in Atlanta, Georgia, the Georgia Aquarium is the largest aquarium in the United States with more than 100,000 animals living in its many exhibits. It is also one of the biggest researchers in aquatic conservation, with a special focus on sea lions, manta rays, beluga whales, Atlantic bottlenose dolphins, African penguins, whale sharks, and coral. Not only does the Georgia Aquarium work hard to study and support biodiversity, they also take big steps toward educating the public on how to contribute positively to the environment. They are recognized as a Class R research facility, meaning they can facilitate scientific research outside of the typical scope of an aquarium, allowing them to further support wildlife conservation.
